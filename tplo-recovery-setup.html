OLIVER'S TPLO RESOURCE CENTER

What recovery setup actually worked for Oliver?

The best setup was flexible, cool, observable, and connected to normal household life.

What this page is: Kristin's documented experience with Oliver, separated from veterinary information and remaining uncertainty.

The setup that worked was a system, not one product.

The recovery area had to manage movement, comfort, temperature, anxiety, supervision, and Oliver's need to remain part of normal family life. That is why it changed during both recoveries.

First recovery

The original pen felt tighter once the bed, cone, bowls, and Oliver were all inside it. The setup gained a quieter fan, a partial cover for security, adjusted bowls, and different bedding. Oliver began receiving supervised freedom and stroller inclusion early because he settled better when he remained part of the household.

Second recovery

I began with a larger pen, a camera, stronger awareness of airflow, and more room to adapt. The pen soon became a flexible nook with walls folded back because Oliver often chose nearby beds or the floor and returned to the enclosed space when storms made it feel useful.

How I would build it now

Enough room

The dog should be able to stand, turn, lie fully extended, use bowls with the cone, and change positions without the pen becoming an obstacle course.

Two safe surfaces

Provide supportive bedding and a cooler non-slip option. Oliver sometimes ignored expensive beds because temperature mattered more than softness.

Safe airflow

The quiet fan was one of the most useful items. It stayed outside the pen with the cord protected.

Camera visibility

A camera reduced unnecessary checking and helped me see whether Oliver was resting, licking, pacing, or choosing a different area.

Partial cover

A cover could make part of the pen feel protected without blocking airflow or making Oliver feel trapped.

Other-dog plan

Baxter remained nearby, but normal walks, play, and excitement had to be managed so Oliver did not repeatedly try to join.
