THE PERSPECTIVE BEHIND THIS RESOURCE

One dog needed TPLO surgery twice, only seven weeks apart.

Oliver's right TPLO was performed on April 16, 2026. He injured the left knee on May 21 and had the second TPLO on June 5. The timing was unusual and forced me to apply what I had just learned while discovering that the same dog could still present a different recovery problem.

The second surgery is not used as a dramatic reveal. It matters because it explains why this resource can compare planning, confinement, incision management, activity, and owner confidence across two real recoveries.

Use this site as practical context

Oliver's experience can help you prepare better questions and notice issues you may not have considered. Your dog's medication, restrictions, follow-up schedule, and return to activity belong to your veterinary team.
