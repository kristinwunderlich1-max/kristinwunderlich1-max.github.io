LESSONS LEARNED

What do I wish I had known before TPLO surgery?

Most of what changed my thinking had very little to do with the surgery itself. It came from living through recovery one ordinary day at a time.

THE BIGGEST LESSON

Recovery was not about finding perfect answers. It was about paying attention.

I expected recovery to be a checklist. Instead it became a series of observations, adjustments, and conversations with my veterinary team.

Looking back, the most valuable habit was documenting what I saw instead of relying on memory.

Prepare, then adapt.

The house I prepared before surgery was only the starting point. Oliver's behavior showed me what needed to change.

Routine matters.

Protecting the knee was only part of recovery. Preserving Oliver's routines helped preserve his confidence.

Participation matters.

The stroller, modified games, and the lake trip all solved the same problem: letting Oliver remain part of life.

Observe before buying.

No product solved everything. Watching how Oliver actually used the environment mattered more than collecting equipment.

Capacity is not clearance.

Oliver often showed he could do more before I was ready. His ability never replaced veterinary guidance.

Small notes become big lessons.

Many of the most useful insights came from details that seemed unimportant when I first wrote them down.

IF I COULD TALK TO MYSELF BEFORE SURGERY

These are the things I would say.

  • You do not need every answer on day one.
  • Ask questions early and write the answers down.
  • Build a recovery environment that can change.
  • Keep comparing today with yesterday.
  • Trust what you are observing, then verify concerns with your veterinary team.
  • Some of the best solutions will be ordinary household adaptations.
  • Protect recovery without forgetting the dog's emotional wellbeing.

THANK YOU

I hope this helps you begin one step ahead.

If these stories help you ask a better question, notice something sooner, or feel less alone, then sharing Oliver's recovery was worthwhile.
