What this page is: Kristin's documented experience with Oliver, separated from veterinary information and remaining uncertainty.

Two separate timelines are easier to use than one combined diary.

These milestones show what happened and when. The guides explain the decisions in more detail.

RIGHT TPLO

First recovery

  1. April 16, 2026

    Right TPLO surgery.

  2. Days 1–4

    Pen recovery, cone, icing, close incision observation, bathroom trips, and repeated comfort adjustments.

  3. April 21–22 · Days 5–6

    Joined walks in the stroller and began supervised time outside the pen with modified low-movement play.

  4. April 25–26 · Days 9–10

    Functionally free-ranging on the main floor enough that couch access became the main management problem.

  5. April 28 · Day 12

    Sutures removed.

  6. April 30 · Day 14

    Left home briefly without the cone; Oliver rested and left the incision alone.

  7. May 5

    Photographed after the early recovery period, using normal household spaces more freely.

LEFT TPLO

Second recovery

  1. May 21, 2026

    Injury during an attempted jump into the SUV.

  2. May 28

    Complete left CCL tear diagnosed.

  3. June 5

    Left TPLO; no meniscus damage reported. Two shoulder masses also removed.

  4. June 5–10

    Larger pen, camera, fan, brown noise, supervised freedom, stroller return, and rapid transition toward a flexible recovery nook.

  5. June 12

    Oliver removed a couple of stitches after I forgot to replace the cone before stepping away.

  6. June 19–26

    Intermittent gait concerns and a period of increased monitoring.

  7. July 10–13

    Lake trip with swimming, varied terrain, exploration, and no dock jumping.

  8. July 16

    Focus shifted toward realistic activity, strength, endurance, and confidence.

For the direct comparison, read What I Did Differently the Second Time.
